The whole point

Your voice is the most personal data you have. So Skryver keeps it on your machine — the speech model and the language model both run locally, and not one byte is sent anywhere.

  • No network calls. Dictation works fully offline — unplug the Wi‑Fi and it's identical.
  • No account, no telemetry. Nothing to sign up for, nothing tracked.
  • Open source. Read every line — it's MIT‑licensed on GitHub.
The whole interaction

Three things, and none of them is “open an app”.

Skryver lives in your system tray and listens for one key. Dictation is the same everywhere — a browser, an email, a chat box, your code editor.

01

Hold the key

Click into any text field, then press and hold F9 (or whatever hotkey you pick).

02

Just talk

Speak naturally — stumble, restate, correct yourself mid‑sentence. Skryver is listening on‑device.

03

Release

Let go, and clean text appears right where your cursor is — punctuated, de‑filler‑ed, ready.

What's inside

Small, quiet, and genuinely useful.

Push‑to‑talk anywhere

One global hotkey works in every app. No window to switch to, no button to click.

On‑device AI cleanup

A local language model fixes grammar, punctuation and filler as you dictate.

“send it to john, sorry i mean jane”
→ “Send it to Jane.”

Fast or accurate

Pick Parakeet for correctness on names and jargon, or Moonshine for near‑instant results.

Personal dictionary

Teach it the names and terms it keeps mishearing — matched case‑insensitively, whole‑word.

Lives in the tray

No window in your face. A tray icon, a warm settings screen when you want it, and that's it.

Yours, offline

No subscription, no sign‑in, no server. It just runs on your computer, forever.

Under the hood

Real models, running on your CPU.

No GPU required. Skryver bundles the speech and language models it needs and resolves them next to the app.

Speech → text
sherpa‑onnx

Parakeet & Moonshine ONNX models handle transcription locally.

Cleanup
llama.cpp

A quantized Qwen2.5‑1.5B instruct model polishes each take.
